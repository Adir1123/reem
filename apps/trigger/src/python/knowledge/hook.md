# Hook Archetypes (Personal Finance Tips)

Hooks are slide 1's job: **stop the scroll, earn the swipe**. This file is
the catalog of hook archetypes (the formulas) plus the rules that govern
when each works. Pass B picks an archetype based on the source content's
shape, then writes the actual Hebrew copy.

Pairs with:
- `copy-patterns-he.md` — concrete approved Hebrew examples per archetype.
- `brand-voice-he.md` — voice rules every hook must obey.
- `bidi-rules.md` — typography for hooks containing English finance terms.

---

## Universal hook rules

Every hook obeys all three. A hook violating any is rejected before
transcreation.

### Rule 1 — Visual-first

The hook is **paired with a single dominant visual** (chart, account
screenshot, money-related hero object). Write the image direction first,
then write the words to fit the visual hierarchy.

### Rule 2 — Specific over abstract

Always concrete. **A real number. A real account type. A real claim.** No
"this powerful tool", no "amazing strategy". If you can swap the strategy
name for any other strategy and the hook still works, the hook is too
generic — rewrite.

| ❌ Generic | ✅ Specific |
|---|---|
| הכלי החדש שמשנה הכל | Roth IRA |
| המון אנשים מפספסים את זה | 70% מפספסים את זה |
| יותר חיסכון בפחות זמן | $5,000 בשנה, אוטומטי |

### Rule 3 — "How I" framing beats "How to"

When a hook frames a tutorial, **personal discovery beats instructional command**.

- ✅ `ככה הפכתי את ה-401k שלי` (how *I* did it)
- ❌ `איך לפתוח 401k` (how *to* do it)

`ככה` carries discovery and personality. `איך` sounds like documentation.
Use `איך` only when the hook is genuinely instructional (rare).

## The archetypes

Each archetype has: name, formula, layout family (per `bidi-rules.md` +
`copy-patterns-he.md`), example Hebrew + English versions, and a "use when"
rule.

---

### A1 — Numbered Roundup ("טופ N")

**Formula**: `[NUMBER]` + `[ENGLISH BRAND TERM]` + `[Hebrew benefit subhead]`

**Layout**: A (three-layer — kicker + main + subhead).

**Use when**: Source content is N items of similar weight (e.g. 5 ETFs to
hold long-term, 3 IRA mistakes).

**Hebrew example**:
> Kicker: `טופ 5`
> Main: `Index Funds`
> Subhead: `שמכפילים את החיסכון לפנסיה`

**English variant**:
> Kicker: `TOP 5`
> Main: `Index Funds`
> Subhead: `That'll 10x Your Retirement Savings`

**Why it works**: Number gives concrete promise. Brand/concept term as
massive headline does the visual work. Subhead delivers the payoff.

---

### A2 — Stat-Shock ("99%")

**Formula**: `[BIG NUMBER / PERCENTAGE]` + `[Hebrew implication]`

**Layout**: A.

**Use when**: There's a genuinely surprising stat about the audience or the
strategy.

**Hebrew example**:
> Main: `70%`
> Subhead: `מהאמריקאים לא מקסמו את ה-401k שלהם`

**Why it works**: A big number sets up an information gap the swipe must close.

**Anti-pattern**: don't fabricate stats. If you don't have a real `70%`,
use a different archetype.

---

### A3 — Bold Transformation ("X הוא לא מה שחשבת")

**Formula**: `[BRAND/CONCEPT]` + Hebrew radical reframe of what it actually is

**Layout**: A.

**Use when**: A common assumption about a strategy is wrong.

**Hebrew example**:
> Main: `Roth IRA`
> Subhead: `הוא לא מה שחשבת`

**Why it works**: Forces the reader to update their mental model.

**Anti-pattern**: clichéd transformations (`...משנה הכל`, `המהפכה האמיתית`)
— banned per `brand-voice-he.md`.

---

### A4 — Audience-Specific ("X ל[Audience]")

**Formula**: `[BRAND/CONCEPT]` + `ל[AUDIENCE-IN-ONE-WORD]`

**Layout**: A.

**Use when**: Targeting a specific professional or life-stage vertical
(self-employed, freelancers, parents, twentysomethings).

**Hebrew example**:
> Main: `Roth IRA`
> Subhead: `לעצמאים`

**Why it works**: Self-selecting hook — the named audience feels seen,
others scroll past (which is fine — wrong audience never converts anyway).

---

### A5 — Mistakes ("N טעויות")

**Formula**: `[NUMBER] טעויות` + `שכולם עושים עם [STRATEGY/ACCOUNT]`

**Layout**: A.

**Use when**: Content covers common errors / anti-patterns.

**Hebrew example**:
> Main: `3 טעויות`
> Subhead: `שכולם עושים עם 401k`

**English variant**: `3 Mistakes Everyone Makes With Their 401k`

**Why it works**: Curiosity (am I making them?) + ego defense (probably not
me, but let me check).

---

### A6 — Personal Transformation ("ככה הפכתי...")

**Formula**: `ככה [VERB] [SCOPE] בעזרת [BRAND/STRATEGY]`

**Layout**: B (long narrative).

**Use when**: First-person tutorial — you actually executed this.

**Hebrew example**:
> `ככה הפכתי את התיק שלי בעזרת 3 ETFs בלבד`

**English variant**:
> `How I rebuilt my portfolio with just 3 ETFs`

**Why it works**: First-person earns trust. Specific scope earns specificity.

**Constraint**: Don't fake first-person. If you didn't actually do it, use
A3 or A1 instead.

---

### A7 — Counter-Intuitive Personal ("הפסקתי X. הסיבה: Y")

**Formula**: `הפסקתי [OLD HABIT]. הסיבה: [NEW THING]`

**Layout**: B.

**Use when**: Content represents a pivot — you did something different than
the obvious advice.

**Hebrew example**:
> `הפסקתי לחסוך עוד ב-IRA. הסיבה: Mega Backdoor.`

**Why it works**: Two-clause structure with em-dash-style colon turn opens
a curiosity loop. The `הסיבה:` colon-pivot is high-conviction native rhythm.

**Variant formulas**:
- `הפסקתי X. עברתי ל-Y.`
- `במשך שנה עשיתי X. עכשיו אני עושה Y.`

---

### A8 — Long First-Person Achievement ("אני הרגע...")

**Formula**: `אני הרגע [VERB] [SCOPE / NUMBER] בעזרת [BRAND/STRATEGY]`

**Layout**: B.

**Use when**: A genuine recent personal milestone the audience would want
to replicate.

**Hebrew example**:
> `אני הרגע סגרתי את ה-401k המקסימום של 2025 בעזרת אוטומציה אחת`

**Why it works**: Stat embedded mid-sentence (not as kicker). Brand/strategy
lands at the end as the punchline. Reads like a real tweet, not a magazine
cover.

**Constraint**: must be true. Specific scope + specific stat are essential.

## Format → archetype map

| Source-content format | Primary archetypes | Layout family |
|---|---|---|
| `deep-dive` (one strategy explained deeply) | A3 (Bold Transformation), A6 (Personal Transformation) | A or B |
| `roundup` (5 things ranked) | A1 (Numbered Roundup), A4 (Audience-Specific) | A |
| `tutorial` (how-to walkthrough) | A6 (How I), A8 (First-Person Achievement) | B preferred |
| `mistakes` (common errors) | A5 (Mistakes) | A |
| `vs` (comparison: Roth vs Traditional, ETF vs mutual fund) | A3 (Bold Transformation — frame the loser as outdated) | A |

## Banned hooks (zero tolerance)

These signal AI-generated / hype content immediately. Reject before transcreation.

- ❌ `מוכן/ה לשנות את המשחק?`
- ❌ `הגיע הזמן ל...`
- ❌ `גלה את הסוד של...`
- ❌ `העלה הילוך`
- ❌ `קח את ה-X שלך לרמה הבאה`
- ❌ `המהפכה האמיתית`
- ❌ `הכלי שישנה את הכל`
- ❌ `המדריך המלא ל...` (overused — only when literally complete and only as a body claim)
- ❌ `5 דברים שלא ידעת על...` (use A2 stat-shock or A5 mistakes — more specific)
- ❌ Generic motivational openers (`האם אתה...?`, `דמיין ש...`, `תאר לעצמך...`)

See `brand-voice-he.md` for the full banned-phrase list.

---

## Hook generation checklist

Before any hook ships:

- [ ] One specific number, account type, or claim is present (Rule 2)
- [ ] If first-person: actually true; if instructional: framed `ככה`/`How I`, not `איך`/`How to` (Rule 3)
- [ ] Maps to one of A1–A8 archetypes (no inventing new shapes silently)
- [ ] Layout family declared (A or B)
- [ ] Word count fits layout: A = main 1–4 words, subhead 4–8 words; B = single line up to 16 words
- [ ] No banned phrases / motivational clichés
- [ ] Pairs with a clear visual concept (no text-only hooks)

If any check fails, regenerate or pick a different archetype.
