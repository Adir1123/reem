# The Carousel Framework

**The 7-slide structure for high-value @personalfinancetips carousels.**

Goal of every carousel: stop the scroll with a strong hook, deliver five concrete tips that are useful on their own, then earn the follow. The five tips, taken together, form a complete answer to the question the hook posed; taken individually, each one stands alone in the Explore feed.

---

## The Structure

| SLIDE | ROLE | PURPOSE | VISUAL ENERGY |
|-------|------|---------|---------------|
| 1 | HOOK | Stop the scroll. Promise specific value. Must work standalone. | **HIGH** |
| 2 | TIP  | Tip #1 — concrete, actionable, standalone. | **MEDIUM** |
| 3 | TIP  | Tip #2 — concrete, actionable, standalone. | **MEDIUM** |
| 4 | TIP  | Tip #3 — the most counter-intuitive or "aha" of the five. | **HIGH** |
| 5 | TIP  | Tip #4 — concrete, actionable, standalone. | **MEDIUM** |
| 6 | TIP  | Tip #5 — strongest closer; the one they'll remember and screenshot. | **MEDIUM** |
| 7 | CTA  | Earn the follow. | **LOW** |

---

## What makes a STRONG hook (slide 1)

A hook earns its slide if it does at least one of:
- Promises a specific quantity (`5 ways to…`, `3 things nobody tells you about…`)
- States a contrarian or counter-intuitive position (`Stop budgeting like this.`)
- Names a specific cost the reader is paying right now (`The $4,000 mistake nobody talks about.`)
- Puts a number, dollar amount, or named tool in the headline itself

Headline patterns that work:
- `5 ways to ___`
- `Stop ___ing like this.`
- `The $___ mistake nobody talks about.`
- `Most people ___ backwards. Here's the fix.`
- `How I ___ in ___ months.`
- `___ saved me $___ this year.`
- `___ rules I follow before I spend $___.`

Patterns that KILL the hook:
- "Did you know…" / "Here's the truth…" / "Want to learn…"
- Any rhetorical question without a specific number or noun
- "Tips & tricks" framing — too generic
- "Important things to know" — vague
- "Let's talk about…" — soft opener
- Anything that needs the body to be understood

The 0.3-second test: a stranger in the Explore feed sees only the headline and the image. Do they stop? If they need the body to understand what the hook is offering, the hook is broken — rewrite.

## What makes a STRONG tip (slides 2-6)

Each TIP slide is one standalone, usable insight. The reader who screenshots only this slide must walk away with something they can act on or remember.

A tip earns its slide if AT LEAST ONE of:
- It contains a concrete number (percentage, dollar amount, ratio, timeframe).
- It names a specific account type, product, tool, or technique.
- It tells the reader a specific action they can take this week.
- It contrasts a wrong way with a right way using specific behaviours.

A tip is filler if:
- It's a generic platitude ("save more", "invest early", "live below your means").
- It restates the hook without adding new information.
- It needs the previous slide's context to make sense.
- It uses "many people", "experts say", or "studies show" without a number.

The screenshot test: someone screenshots only this slide and forwards it. Does it land alone? If not, rewrite.

Layout per TIP slide:
- **Eyebrow** — tip marker + short label (e.g. `TIP 01 · AUTOMATE`, `TIP 03 · TAX HACK`).
- **Headline** — the tip itself, 6 words max. The gold italic word should land on the action verb or the dollar amount.
- **Body** — **2–3 short lines** that earn their space:
  1. **The anchor line** — the concrete number, tool, account, or exact step the headline refers to.
  2. **The why or how line(s)** — pick one and commit:
     - **Why** — the reason this tip works (a mechanism, a number, a specific cost the reader pays without it).
     - **How** — the literal next move, in 1–2 steps the reader can do this week.
  Don't pad. If line 3 is just a softer restatement of line 2, drop it. Three sharp lines beat two sharp lines + filler. One sharp line beats three filler lines.
- **`step_number`** — set to `"01"`…`"05"` matching the tip number, so the renderer can show the chapter marker.

Tip variety across the five slots:
- At least one tip should be a specific number / dollar amount.
- At least one should be a specific tool, account, or product.
- At least one should be a contrarian "stop doing X" reframe — this is usually slot 4 (HIGH energy).

Don't repeat the same shape across all five tips. Mix: number-tip, action-tip, contrarian-tip, tool-tip, mindset-tip.

## CTA (slide 7)

- Solid brand navy. No background image (`ref_image: null`).
- Centered. Gold pill with `@personalfinancetips`.
- Headline + body together: thank, hint at what's next, ask for save / follow.

---

## Universal rules

- Headlines: **6 words max**, concrete, specific.
- Body: **3 lines max** per slide.
- One idea per slide. If a second sentence is needed to explain it, the idea is too complex.
- Plain language. No jargon, no fake urgency, no shame, no 🚨, no "they don't want you to know".
- Cut `honestly`, `literally`, `the truth is`, `actually`. Say the thing.
- Never copy a transcript sentence verbatim. Compress and reframe.

---

## Visual storytelling

Match visual energy to narrative energy:
- **HOOK** — the most dramatic, high-contrast image you have. Stops the scroll before anyone reads.
- **TIPs (slides 2, 3, 5, 6)** — warm, textural, focused. Each tip's image should feel related to its action (a budget app screen for an automate-tip, coins for a savings-tip, etc.).
- **TIP 4 (HIGH)** — most conceptual or unexpected image of the carousel. Marks the "aha" tip.
- **CTA** — no image. Solid brand background. The visual shift signals this is the ask.
