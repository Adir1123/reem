# The Carousel Framework

**The 7-slide structure behind every high-performing carousel.**

---

## The Structure

Every carousel follows the same narrative arc. Each slide has a defined role. The content changes, the structure doesn't.

| SLIDE | ROLE | PURPOSE | VISUAL ENERGY |
|-------|------|---------|---------------|
| 1 | HOOK | Stop the scroll. Provoke curiosity or tension. Must work standalone. | **HIGH** — Dramatic image, bold headline |
| 2 | CONTEXT | Frame the problem. Why this matters right now. | **LOW** — Clean, text-only, breathing room |
| 3 | BUILD | Core insight #1. One idea only. Build momentum. | **MEDIUM** — Warm, textural, alternate dark/light |
| 4 | BUILD | Core insight #2. One idea only. Keep building. | **MEDIUM** — Alternate from slide 3 |
| 5 | TENSION | The reframe. Challenge the assumption. Create the aha. | **HIGH** — Conceptual, abstract, unexpected |
| 6 | PAYOFF | The key takeaway. Deliver the resolution they came for. | **MEDIUM** — Clean, resolved, open |
| 7 | CTA | Earn the follow. Give them a reason to stay. | **LOW** — Solid brand bg, accent headline |

---

## The Rules

- Never copy source material word for word. Restructure for the format.
- Each slide must make sense if someone sees it on its own in Explore.
- One idea per slide. If you need a second sentence to explain it, it's too complex.
- Tension before payoff. Always. The carousel earns the insight, never gives it away early.
- Slide 1 is judged alone. It must provoke enough curiosity to justify a swipe.
- Headlines: 6 words maximum.
- Body text: 3 lines maximum per slide.
- Plain language. No jargon. Your audience includes non-native English speakers.

---

## Visual Storytelling

If you're using images, match the visual energy to the narrative energy of each slide. Don't just drop images behind text randomly — that's decoration, not storytelling.

- **Hook (Slide 1)** — Your most dramatic, high-contrast image. This stops the scroll before anyone reads a word.
- **Context (Slide 2)** — No image or very subtle. Clean breathing room makes the hook hit harder by contrast.
- **Build (Slides 3–4)** — Warm, textural images. Close-ups, detail shots. Visual momentum pulling you deeper.
- **Tension (Slide 5)** — Most conceptual or striking image. Should feel different from everything before it.
- **Payoff (Slide 6)** — Clean, resolved, open. Clarity after tension.
- **CTA (Slide 7)** — No image. Solid brand background. The visual shift signals this is the ask.
